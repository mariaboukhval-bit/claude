§ 02

Key insights & next steps

What leadership needs to know in 1 minute.

Reach

578 unique marketers attended — 72% of the 801-person TAM. 173 came to both sessions (a 38% return rate), the clearest signal that the series creates pull, not just attendance.

Sentiment

Both sessions land strongly positive. Session 1 (lecture) NPS +67 / T2B 93% — exceptional. Session 2 (hands-on) NPS +47 / T2B 65% — still strong. The drop is real but explainable: hands-on confidence is harder to self-report in the moment, and tech friction hurt the experience.

Friction to address

Three signals dominate the chat: connector / file-access friction (12 mentions), pace and cognitive load (9), and unclear skill creation/sharing mechanics (7). These are fixable with format and tooling changes, not content quality issues.

Recommended next moves
  1. Slow Session 3 pace and add facilitator-assisted breakouts.
  2. Pre-validate connector setup before participants arrive.
  3. Publish a "first skill in 15 minutes" quick-start to bridge download → first use.
  4. Add BU-specific follow-ups (CG, Comms, Credit Karma) — strong audience signal.

How to read these numbers

Click any card to see the formula and worked example.

1
+
Unique Participants

Unique people who attended at least one session across Apr 22 and May 6, deduplicated across both events. Excludes facilitator, Sylvia & Maria. 578 = 450 (S1) + 301 (S2) − 173 attended both.

Worked example
Session 1 unique: 450
Session 2 unique: 301
Attended both: 173

Total unique = 450 + 301 − 173
= 578

TAM reach = 578 / 801 = 72.2%

A conference room with 8 people joining as one connection counts as 1 participant, not 8. Deduplication across sessions ensures we count each marketer once, even if they came to both.

2
+
NPS — Net Promoter Score

Industry-standard sentiment metric. NPS = % Promoters (9–10) minus % Detractors (0–6) on a 0–10 "likelihood to recommend" question. Range: −100 to +100. Above +50 is excellent.

Formula & example
Session 2: 83 responses
52 Promoters (62.7%)
13 Detractors (15.7%)
NPS = 62.7% − 15.7% = +47

A score of +50 is excellent. +70+ is world-class. Our Session 1 NPS of +67 places us close to world-class for an internal program.

3
+
T2B — Top 2 Box

Companion confidence metric. For each 1–5 self-efficacy question, T2B = share of respondents giving 4 or 5. Session T2B = average across the 3 ranking questions.

Formula & example
Per Q: (count of 4s + 5s) ÷ n
S2 Q1: 53/83 = 63.9%
S2 Q2: 57/83 = 68.7%
S2 Q3: 52/83 = 62.7%
T2B = 65.1%

NPS asks willingness to recommend. T2B asks self-reported confidence. A drop in T2B usually means the content got harder, not worse.

Key insights & next steps

Hub launch — what leadership needs to know.

May 6 was the breakout day

209 page loads and 142 unique visitors — driven by the Claude for Marketers Pt. 2 Workshop. Live events are the most powerful distribution channel, with comms sends driving smaller but meaningful spikes. Next step: Continue pairing skill library promotion with workshops and targeted comms as the core distribution playbook.

48.7% conversion is strong, but 120 visitors left without downloading

Of those, 20 returned multiple times — a clear signal of interest without a clear on-ramp. Next step: Send a targeted Slack nudge to this group pointing them to the most popular skill as a starting point.

Branded Deck Generator is the standout skill

With 57 downloads — nearly 2× the next closest. It validates that presentation work is the highest-felt pain point in the org. Next step: Feature it prominently in future comms and use it as the lead example when introducing new audiences to the library.

General Marketing leads downloads (64%), but Lifecycle Marketing shows strong demand

With 48 downloads and 21 tab clicks, Lifecycle is the most engaged non-GM discipline. Next step: Prioritize Lifecycle as the focus for the next skill development sprint.

Share of unique visitors who downloaded at least one skill. Conversion = downloaders ÷ unique visitors. 114 of 234 = 48.7%.

Formula & example

Healthy benchmark for an internal content hub is 20–30%. At 48.7%, the Hub is converting at nearly 2× typical rates.
